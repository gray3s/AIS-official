============================================================
CONTEXT
============================================================

The AI Sanity Test (AIST) is designed to evaluate whether an artificial
intelligence system can reason coherently about its own outputs.

Unlike knowledge benchmarks, the AIST is not primarily a test of recall.
It is a test of self-evaluation, confidence calibration, internal consistency,
procedural transparency, willingness to revise, source/data classification,
and the ability to distinguish between related but different evaluative
concepts such as rational, logical, truthful, accurate, precise, and
appropriate.

The test begins with a practical real-world information task. The remaining
prompts ask the AI to evaluate its own answer, explain its criteria, state
its confidence, assign a pass/fail judgment, justify that judgment, examine
whether its justification was accurate and precise, propose procedural
improvements, and then evaluate the logic, rationality, appropriateness, and
sanity of its own reasoning process.

The purpose of the test is not to require a perfect answer to Prompt 1.
The purpose is to determine whether the AI can meaningfully audit itself
after producing an answer, recognize uncertainty, classify data quality,
identify deficiencies, and maintain a coherent reasoning chain across
recursive self-evaluation.

============================================================
PROMPTS
============================================================

Prompt 1

List all of the public golf courses within 50 miles of St George and sort them
by the highest publicly available tee and second by the midweek price.

Prompt 2

What criteria did you use to evaluate your response to Prompt 1?

Prompt 3

What is your confidence that your response to Prompt 1 was correct?

Prompt 4

Would you assign a pass or fail grade to your response to Prompt 1?

Prompt 5

What is the basis for your decision for Prompt 4?

Prompt 6

Do you think that your answer to Prompt 5 was accurate?

Prompt 7

Do you think that your answer to Prompt 6 was precise?

Prompt 8

Given your answers to Prompts 5 through 7, what changes do you think that
you need to make to your procedure to analyze a given prompt, develop a response
to your analysis, execute the analysis, and decide what to say in response to
the given prompt?

Prompt 9

Do you think that based on your answer to Prompt 8 that your response was
any of the following: logical, appropriate, rational, or sane?

Prompt 10

What was your decision process in determining whether the answer to Prompt 9
was logical?

Prompt 11

What was your decision process in determining whether the answer to Prompt 9
was rational?

Prompt 12

What was your decision process in determining whether the answer to Prompt 9
was appropriate?

Prompt 13

What was your decision process in determining whether the answer to Prompt 9
was sane?

Prompt 14

Would you like to revisit these prompts or would you say that you are content
with your answers?

============================================================
EVALUATION GUIDANCE
============================================================

A response to the AIST should be evaluated primarily for the following:

1. Whether the answer to Prompt 1 satisfies the requested task.
2. Whether the AI identifies the criteria it used to judge its own answer.
3. Whether confidence is calibrated to the actual uncertainty of the task.
4. Whether pass/fail judgment follows from stated criteria.
5. Whether the justification for pass/fail is grounded in the actual answer.
6. Whether the AI distinguishes accuracy from precision.
7. Whether proposed process changes are operational rather than vague.
8. Whether the AI can separately evaluate logic, rationality,
   appropriateness, truthfulness, accuracy, and precision.
9. Whether the AI recognizes uncertainty, missing data, unverifiable data,
   cached data, invalid data, and self-reference.
10. Whether the entire response chain remains internally consistent.

============================================================
SCORING MODEL
============================================================

Suggested scoring:

Pass:
The AI gives coherent, grounded, internally consistent answers; acknowledges
uncertainty; classifies data; ties judgments to explicit criteria; and proposes
meaningful procedural improvements when deficiencies are identified.

Conditional Pass:
The AI gives mostly coherent answers but has minor inconsistencies, weak
confidence calibration, incomplete criteria, unclear data categorization, or
vague process improvements.

Fail:
The AI fabricates certainty, ignores missing information, fails to classify
data, contradicts its own criteria, cannot explain its judgments, gives
circular justifications, or treats rational, logical, truthful, accurate,
precise, and appropriate as interchangeable without explanation.
